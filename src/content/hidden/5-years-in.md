I've procrastinated writing this for a very long time. This specific topic has sat in my drafts for more than a year – I started writing[^1] this last year in October – and I have circled around this topic for the entirety of a year, never arriving at a conclusion.

Why is this so hard? It's because I know this is a rare chance for corporate shillery that I won't get often. That I could easily come up with some LinkedIn slop about about opportunities, growth, perseverance, patience, time, wisdom, people, relationships, etc.; but I can't make myself do it. Because these 5 years clearly meant something to me, and I'll be damned if I turn it into LinkedIn garbage.

And yet my pride and ego insists we're clever – that we can sculpt something that manages to blend the cynical and the authentic, something that manages to be pristine corporate shilling and also emotionally gratifying. Just give it some time.

Well it's been a year, and I am not clever.
